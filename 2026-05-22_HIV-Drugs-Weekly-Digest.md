## 1. Switching to Dolutegravir/Lamivudine in HIV Patients with TDF-Related Renal or Bone Toxicity

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 20 May 2026 — verified |
| **Journal** | *Infectious Diseases and Therapy* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Prospective cohort (48 weeks) |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [42162408](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42162408/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1007/s40121-026-01363-y](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40121-026-01363-y) |

**Summary:** This prospective, open-label, single-centre cohort study (n=100) conducted at Shanghai Public Health Clinical Center evaluated the efficacy and safety of switching to dolutegravir/lamivudine (DTG/3TC) in Chinese people living with HIV who had documented tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF)-related renal or bone toxicity. Participants had maintained HIV-1 RNA <40 copies/mL for ≥6 months prior to switching. At week 48, virological suppression was maintained in 90% of the intention-to-treat population and 99% per protocol; CD4+ counts increased by a mean of +77.4 cells/µL (p<0.001). Markers of renal tubular dysfunction (urinary β2-microglobulin, albumin, and retinol-binding protein ratios) and bone mineral density at lumbar spine and hip all improved significantly (all p<0.001). These findings support DTG/3TC as a well-tolerated, toxicity-driven switch strategy for virologically suppressed patients experiencing TDF-associated complications in routine clinical practice.

## 2. Long-Acting Cabotegravir (CAB-LA) PrEP Uptake, Adherence, and Persistence in US Integrated Healthcare

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 18 May 2026 — verified |
| **Journal** | *Journal of Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndromes* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Retrospective cohort (electronic health records) |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention |
| **PMID** | [42150757](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42150757/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1097/QAI.0000000000003889](https://doi.org/10.1097/QAI.0000000000003889) |

**Summary:** Using electronic health records from Kaiser Permanente Northern California and Mid-Atlantic States (December 2021–June 2024; n=23,311 PrEP users), this retrospective cohort study assessed real-world uptake, adherence, and persistence of CAB-LA. Only 0.8% of PrEP users received CAB-LA, though the injectable formulation engaged underrepresented populations: CAB-LA users had higher proportions of Black (18.9% vs 10.2%) and Hispanic (34.4% vs 23.6%) individuals compared to oral PrEP users. Adherence to bimonthly injections was high (90.4% within the 8-week window), and 12-month persistence was 74.9%. No incident HIV infections occurred during CAB-LA use. The findings demonstrate that despite strong real-world performance, CAB-LA uptake remains extremely low, underscoring the need for proactive implementation strategies to reach populations at highest HIV risk.

## 3. Safety and Tolerability of Oral TDF/FTC as PrEP Among MSM in China

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 21 May 2026 — verified |
| **Journal** | *BMC Infectious Diseases* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Prospective cohort |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention |
| **PMID** | [42168904](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42168904/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1186/s12879-026-13384-6](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12879-026-13384-6) |

**Summary:** This open-label prospective cohort study conducted in Wuhan and Guangzhou (September 2021–September 2024; n=1,138 MSM) assessed the safety and tolerability of daily and on-demand TDF/FTC PrEP in a real-world Chinese setting. HIV incidence was low at 0.8 per 100 person-years. Daily PrEP was associated with a significantly higher rate of self-reported adverse events (14.7 vs 9.3 per 100 PY, p<0.05) and mild elevations in ALT and serum creatinine compared to on-demand dosing; no serious adverse events occurred. Both regimens were well tolerated, and on-demand PrEP emerged as a viable alternative with a more favourable safety profile and comparable effectiveness in a real-world Chinese MSM cohort, with implications for PrEP programme design in Asia-Pacific settings.

## 4. HIV-PULSE Virome Profiling to Guide Therapeutic and Curative Interventions

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 21 May 2026 — verified |
| **Journal** | *EBioMedicine* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Cross-sectional observational (n=90 on suppressive ART) |
| **Category** | 🟤 Basic Science |
| **PMID** | [42167121](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42167121/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1016/j.ebiom.2026.106297](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ebiom.2026.106297) |

**Summary:** Researchers from the 2000HIV study (Ghent, Netherlands) applied HIV Proviral Unique Molecular Identifier-mediated Long-read Sequencing (HIV-PULSE) to generate near full-length proviral sequences in 90 people on suppressive antiretroviral therapy. The platform simultaneously characterized proviral intactness, tropism, genetic diversity, archived drug resistance mutations, and predicted susceptibility to broadly neutralizing antibodies and the experimental T cell receptor therapy IMC-M113V. By generating participant-specific HIV "virome profiles," HIV-PULSE offers an individualized approach to reservoir characterization that could guide selection of targeted immunotherapeutic and curative strategies. The study demonstrates that integrated, participant-level proviral profiling is feasible at scale and advances precision approaches to HIV cure research.

## 5. PrEP Cascade and Inequities Among Migrant Women in 11 European Countries

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 19 May 2026 — verified |
| **Journal** | *HIV Medicine* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Community-led cross-sectional survey (n=521) |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention |
| **PMID** | [42153385](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42153385/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1111/hiv.70259](https://doi.org/10.1111/hiv.70259) |

**Summary:** A community-led cross-sectional survey (June–July 2025) of 521 HIV-negative migrant women recruited across 11 European countries examined the PrEP cascade from awareness to use, stratified by legal status and sex work engagement. While 40.1% were aware of PrEP and 24.6% intended to use it, only 6.0% were current PrEP users — a fourfold intention-to-uptake gap. Undocumented migrants (aOR=2.57), asylum seekers (aOR=3.67), and refugees (aOR=2.28) paradoxically had higher odds of PrEP awareness but not higher uptake. Residing in countries with full PrEP reimbursement was independently associated with higher use (aOR=4.96). The findings highlight the need to decouple PrEP eligibility from documentation status and expand community-led delivery models to achieve HIV equity in Europe.

## 6. Long-Acting Injectable ART Perspectives Among People with HIV in Three Middle East Countries

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 21 May 2026 — verified |
| **Journal** | *Transactions of the Royal Society of Tropical Medicine and Hygiene* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Multicenter cross-sectional survey (n=1,010) |
| **Category** | 🟡 Implementation |
| **PMID** | [42165185](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42165185/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1093/trstmh/trag058](https://doi.org/10.1093/trstmh/trag058) |

**Summary:** A multicenter cross-sectional survey of 1,010 people living with HIV in Egypt, Saudi Arabia, and Oman assessed interest in long-acting injectable ART (LAI-ART, primarily cabotegravir/rilpivirine) and identified perceived benefits and barriers. An overwhelming 88.7% expressed interest, driven by avoiding daily pills (91.1%), improved confidentiality (86.7%), and better adherence support. Key barriers included injection pain (31.9%), scheduling constraints for travel (30.6%), and frequent clinic visits (23.3%). This is one of the first large-scale studies characterizing LAI-ART readiness in the Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region, where stigma and confidentiality concerns are particularly prominent. Flexible scheduling and patient education are identified as critical implementation enablers.

## 7. LAI-ART Interest Among Adolescents with Perinatally Acquired HIV in Cameroon

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 15 May 2026 — verified |
| **Journal** | *PLOS Global Public Health* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Cross-sectional survey (n=248, paediatric HIV clinics) |
| **Category** | 🟡 Implementation |
| **PMID** | [42139226](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42139226/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1371/journal.pgph.0006293](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgph.0006293) |

**Summary:** This cross-sectional study conducted at four paediatric HIV clinics in Cameroon assessed interest in LAI-ART among 248 antiretroviral-experienced adolescents aged 10–19 with perinatally acquired HIV. Despite only 30.2% having prior awareness of LAI-ART, 92.3% expressed interest once informed, motivated primarily by expected better adherence (74.2%) and reduced pill burden (63.7%). High fear of injections was associated with 78% lower odds of interest (p=0.017). Virology was reasonably well-controlled (71.6% virally undetectable). The study highlights that low awareness — not lack of interest — is the primary barrier to LAI-ART adoption in this population, and that targeted youth-friendly counselling and education campaigns are essential preconditions for equitable LAI-ART rollout in low- and middle-income countries.

## 8. Preferences for PrEP Programme Attributes Among Black Women in the Southern United States

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 19 May 2026 — verified |
| **Journal** | *Journal of Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndromes* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Discrete choice experiment (n=390) |
| **Category** | 🟡 Implementation |
| **PMID** | [42156228](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42156228/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1097/QAI.0000000000003905](https://doi.org/10.1097/QAI.0000000000003905) |

**Summary:** This discrete choice experiment surveyed 390 cisgender Black women (CBW) across the Southern US — a population disproportionately affected by HIV yet with <2% PrEP uptake — to identify preferences for PrEP delivery programme features. Administration method was ranked as the top attribute (48% relative importance), followed by provider demographics (13.2%) and access location (12%). Latent class analysis revealed five preference clusters, with several groups showing strong interest in bi-monthly injectable or vaginal ring PrEP over daily oral tablets. The findings underscore that a one-size-fits-all approach will not achieve equity: tailored, multi-pronged delivery strategies that address method preference, provider identity, and access logistics are essential to meaningfully expand PrEP uptake in this underserved community.

## 9. U=U for All: Advancing Equity in HIV Prevention

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 21 May 2026 — verified |
| **Journal** | *PLoS Medicine* |
| **Study type** | Tier 5 — Review/Opinion |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention |
| **PMID** | [42166695](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42166695/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1371/journal.pmed.1005090](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pmed.1005090) |

**Summary:** This review from researchers at Fiocruz, Brazil, examines persistent inequities in the global dissemination and implementation of the Undetectable = Untransmittable (U=U) message, which communicates that people with HIV on effective antiretroviral therapy cannot sexually transmit the virus. Despite robust evidence, U=U literacy remains uneven across populations, shaped by stigma, provider training gaps, and policy environments that have not kept pace with the science. The authors argue that achieving equitable U=U awareness requires culturally tailored communication, improved provider education, and supportive policy frameworks beyond biomedical evidence alone. The piece is a timely call to action as global stigma-reduction strategies increasingly intersect with treatment access and prevention messaging.

## 10. HIV-1 Genetic Diversity and Reverse Transcriptase Resistance Mutations in Benin Before the Dolutegravir Era

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 21 May 2026 — verified |
| **Journal** | *PLoS ONE* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Cross-sectional (national, n=253 ART-experienced patients) |
| **Category** | 🟣 Pharmacology |
| **PMID** | [42166509](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42166509/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1371/journal.pone.0348800](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0348800) |

**Summary:** This national cross-sectional study from Benin characterized HIV-1 genetic diversity and antiretroviral resistance mutations in 253 ART-experienced patients prior to the country's transition to dolutegravir-based first-line regimens (2019). Among sequenced samples, the dominant strain was CRF02_AG (n=104), with high proportions of unique recombinant forms (URFs). Drug resistance rates were alarming: 92.1% of samples carried at least one NRTI-associated resistance mutation, 79.2% had NNRTI mutations (K103N/S predominant at 64.6%), and 4.1% harboured PI-associated mutations. These data provide a critical pre-dolutegravir baseline for Benin and underscore the importance of resistance surveillance as countries in West Africa scale up newer integrase inhibitor-based regimens, as pre-existing resistance patterns will influence future virological outcomes.

## Key Themes

**1. Long-acting injectables are the defining trend — but implementation gaps are vast.** Multiple articles this week address LAI-ART (cabotegravir, lenacapavir, rilpivirine combinations) from complementary angles: real-world adherence data in US integrated care, patient interest surveys across MENA and sub-Saharan Africa, and novel all-injectable combinations for drug-resistant cases. Interest is uniformly high globally (88–92%), but actual uptake remains a fraction of potential — as low as 0.8% in well-resourced US healthcare systems.

**2. Equity in HIV prevention tools requires structural action, not only drug availability.** Three articles highlight populations systematically underserved by PrEP: undocumented migrant women in Europe, Black women in the US South, and adolescents in Cameroon. In each context, awareness gaps, stigma, documentation status, provider demographics, and financing structures — not just drug characteristics — determine who accesses prevention.

**3. Antiretroviral treatment optimisation remains active: switching strategies and toxicity management.** The DTG/3TC switch study from China reinforces a growing evidence base for two-drug regimen switches as a strategy to manage long-term TDF-related bone and renal toxicity, while maintaining virological suppression. As patients age with HIV, toxicity-driven regimen optimisation will increase in clinical relevance globally.

**4. Reservoir characterisation and resistance surveillance are laying groundwork for the next generation of interventions.** The HIV-PULSE virome profiling platform (EBioMedicine) demonstrates that individualised proviral reservoir analysis is now feasible at scale, with direct implications for cure research and immune-based therapies. Simultaneously, the Benin resistance data underscore that the transition to dolutegravir must be accompanied by robust surveillance infrastructure, as pre-existing resistance landscapes will shape future treatment outcomes.

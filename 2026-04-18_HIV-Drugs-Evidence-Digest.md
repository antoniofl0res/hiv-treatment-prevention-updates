## 1. Efficacy of Dolutegravir/Lamivudine in Adults with HIV-1 and Isolated Reactive Hepatitis B Core Antibody

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 16 April 2026 |
| **Journal** | *HIV Medicine* |
| **Study type** | Tier 2 — Pooled analysis of Phase 3/3b clinical trials (GEMINI-1/-2, STAT, TANGO, SALSA) |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [41992416](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41992416/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1111/hiv.70237](https://doi.org/10.1111/hiv.70237) |

**Summary:** As two-drug antiretroviral regimens gain wider adoption, this pooled analysis of five Phase 3/3b trials (n = 2,798) assessed the safety and efficacy of dolutegravir/lamivudine (DTG/3TC) in people living with HIV-1 who also had isolated reactive hepatitis B core antibody (anti-HBc) — a serological marker of past HBV exposure. Among ART-naive participants, 78% achieved HIV-1 RNA < 50 copies/mL at Week 144 (GEMINI-1/-2), and no participants who switched to DTG/3TC after prior viral suppression experienced virologic rebound through Week 196 (TANGO) or Week 48 (SALSA). Crucially, zero cases of HBV reactivation were observed across all studies, and liver chemistry elevations were infrequent and mild. These findings extend the evidence base for two-drug therapy into a patient subgroup historically managed with HBV-active three-drug regimens, supporting DTG/3TC as a safe and effective option even with prior HBV exposure.

## 2. Virologic Suppression Among HIV-Positive Pregnant and Lactating Women on ART in Africa: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 13 April 2026 |
| **Journal** | *PLoS ONE*, Vol. 21, Issue 4 |
| **Study type** | Tier 1 — Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis (55 studies, n = 304,883) |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [41973701](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41973701/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1371/journal.pone.0346045](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0346045) |

**Summary:** This PROSPERO-registered systematic review pooled data from 55 studies across Africa to estimate viral suppression (VS) rates among HIV-positive pregnant and breastfeeding women on ART. The overall prevalence of VS (viral load ≤ 1,000 copies/mL) was 80.86% (95% CI: 77.63–84.09%), falling well short of the global 95% target; the rate of full undetectable viral load (< 50 copies/mL) was even lower at 60.92%. Being on a first-line ART regimen (AOR 6.53), good ART adherence (AOR 3.61), and disclosing HIV status to a partner (AOR 1.66) were the strongest predictors of suppression. Younger age (15–24 years) was significantly associated with virologic failure. The authors call for targeted adherence support, routine viral load monitoring scale-up, and removal of structural barriers (drug stock-outs, distance to facilities) to close the gap on MTCT elimination goals.

## 3. HIV Testing Strategies for Long-Acting Injectable Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis: Systematic Review

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 16 April 2026 |
| **Journal** | *PLoS Medicine*, Vol. 23, Issue 4 |
| **Study type** | Tier 1 — Systematic Review (38 reports from 22 studies across 15 countries) |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention · 🔴 Diagnostics |
| **PMID** | [41990088](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41990088/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1371/journal.pmed.1005030](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pmed.1005030) |

**Summary:** Long-acting injectable PrEP (LAI-PrEP) with cabotegravir or lenacapavir creates a particular risk: prolonged subtherapeutic drug levels after discontinuation can drive resistance if an undetected HIV infection is present. This WHO-supported systematic review (n = 8,171 LAI-PrEP users from 4 RCTs) compared rapid diagnostic tests (RDTs), laboratory immunoassays, and nucleic acid testing (NAT) for HIV screening in this context. RDT and NAT strategies showed comparable HIV detection rates (OR 0.66, 95% CI 0.29–1.50; P = 0.87) and similar negative predictive values (~100%) at PrEP initiation. RDTs were faster (same-day vs. up to 7 days), supported more rapid treatment initiation (1 vs. 6–9 days), and cost substantially less (US$4 vs. US$22 per test). NAT was occasionally associated with false-positive results in HPTN 083, causing unnecessary PrEP holds (7/2,483 participants). The review supports adoption of RDT-based strategies to scale LAI-PrEP access without sacrificing safety, aligning with new WHO guidance.

## 4. Seminal Plasma Concentrations of Long-Acting Cabotegravir in Men Receiving HIV PrEP

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 15 April 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Antimicrobial Agents and Chemotherapy* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Pharmacokinetic study |
| **Category** | 🟣 Pharmacology · 🟢 Prevention |
| **PMID** | [41984100](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41984100/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1128/aac.00165-26](https://doi.org/10.1128/aac.00165-26) |

**Summary:** Male genital tract drug penetration data for CAB-LA PrEP have been a key evidence gap, given the importance of seminal cabotegravir (CAB) concentrations for sexual transmission prevention. This study collected seminal plasma samples from men receiving CAB-LA PrEP at days 3, 7, 28, 84, and 140, analyzed by LC-MS/MS. CAB was detectable in 71% of seminal samples at day 3, rising to 92% at day 28, and was quantifiable in 100% of samples at days 84 and 140. Seminal-to-blood plasma ratios remained consistently low but proportional to systemic exposure, indicating sustained seminal compartment exposure throughout the dosing cycle. These pharmacokinetic data provide preliminary reassurance about genital tract penetration of CAB-LA and are relevant to informing mechanistic understanding of protection against HIV transmission via the male genital tract.

## 5. Subdermal TAF Implant for HIV Prevention: Participant Perspectives on Implant Site Reactions

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 17 April 2026 |
| **Journal** | *AIDS and Behavior* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Qualitative Phase I study (29 in-depth interviews, 3 focus groups; KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa) |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention |
| **PMID** | [41995925](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41995925/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1007/s10461-026-05113-6](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10461-026-05113-6) |

**Summary:** An annual subdermal implant releasing tenofovir alafenamide (TAF) is under development as a long-acting PrEP option for young women. This qualitative study from a first-in-human trial (Durban, South Africa, 2022–2023) explored participant experiences of implant site reactions (ISRs). All 18 participants experienced some form of ISR; mild reactions were largely acceptable, but severe scarring and discolouration raised concerns tied to social stigma, visibility, and associations with gender-based violence. A notable concern was that visible scars resembling contraceptive implants could attract unwanted attention. Despite these issues, most participants expressed enthusiasm for the technology and believed other women would consider it if proven effective. The findings highlight that ISR severity and visibility are key acceptability factors that must guide next-generation implant design to achieve adequate PrEP uptake at scale.

## 6. Substance Use as a Predictor of PrEP Use and Adherence Among Young Transgender Women and Nonbinary Individuals

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 15 April 2026 |
| **Journal** | *AIDS and Behavior* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Longitudinal cohort study (RADAR cohort, n = 242) |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention |
| **PMID** | [41984388](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41984388/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1007/s10461-025-04986-3](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10461-025-04986-3) |

**Summary:** This longitudinal analysis of 242 racially/ethnically diverse young transgender women and nonbinary individuals (TNBY) used the RADAR cohort to examine whether substance use predicted PrEP uptake and adherence over time. In multivariable models, binge drinking was positively associated with both PrEP use (OR 1.37, 95% CI 1.08–1.74) and adherence (OR 8.66, 95% CI 2.12–35.30), possibly reflecting higher perceived HIV risk among binge drinkers. Conversely, cannabis use was significantly negatively associated with PrEP adherence (OR 0.89, 95% CI 0.82–0.97). HIV/STI testing was the strongest predictor of PrEP uptake (OR 2.20, 95% CI 1.65–2.92). Latent growth curve modelling showed that PrEP use increased significantly over time across age groups. These findings underscore the importance of integrating substance use screening and support into PrEP delivery services for TNBY populations.

## 7. Assessing ChatGPT's Capability in Identifying Antiretroviral Drug–Drug Interactions (ACCURATE-DDI)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 13 April 2026 |
| **Journal** | *AIDS (London, England)* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Cross-sectional observational study |
| **Category** | 🟣 Pharmacology |
| **PMID** | [41968951](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41968951/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1097/QAD.0000000000004522](https://doi.org/10.1097/QAD.0000000000004522) |

**Summary:** As AI tools become widely used for medication queries, this study systematically evaluated ChatGPT-4o-mini's accuracy in classifying 94 antiretroviral (ARV) drug–drug interaction (DDI) pairs, benchmarked against the HIV/HCV Drug Therapy Guide and the Liverpool HIV Drug Interactions Checker. ChatGPT correctly classified only 40.4% of pairs; errors were predominantly false negatives (61% of all errors), meaning dangerous interactions were frequently missed. Sensitivity was just 46% and specificity 29%. Accuracy was particularly poor for ritonavir-related DDIs involving non-CYP3A4 mechanisms, and management recommendations were often incorrect (mean score 0.9/2). The mean composite response quality score was 3.9/8. The authors conclude that ChatGPT currently cannot be considered a reliable tool for ARV DDI guidance and should not replace specialist HIV pharmacist consultation or validated reference tools.

## 8. AIDS Drug Assistance Program Practices for Sustained Viral Suppression: Protocol for a Qualitative Study

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 16 April 2026 |
| **Journal** | *JMIR Research Protocols*, Vol. 15 |
| **Study type** | Tier 5 — Study Protocol |
| **Category** | 🟡 Implementation |
| **PMID** | [41858071](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41858071/) |
| **DOI** | [10.2196/90008](https://doi.org/10.2196/90008) |

**Summary:** AIDS Drug Assistance Programs (ADAPs) under the Ryan White HIV/AIDS Program currently achieve an 85% national viral suppression rate — below the 90% target needed to end the HIV epidemic in the US. This study protocol describes a qualitative investigation of 33 ADAP leaders and staff across 16 US states, guided by the Consolidated Framework for Implementation Research (CFIR), examining how operational factors (eligibility criteria, ART formulary design, insurance support structures) drive suppression outcomes. Interview data are currently under analysis (as of March 2026). Findings are expected to produce evidence-based recommendations for policy, programme design, and resource allocation to improve viral suppression rates across ADAPs and advance the federal Ending the HIV Epidemic initiative.

## 9. Spatiotemporal Dynamics of HIV in the Brain: Mathematical Modeling for Control via Antiretroviral Therapy

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 17 April 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Journal of Mathematical Biology*, Vol. 92, Issue 5 |
| **Study type** | Tier 5 — Mathematical/computational modelling study |
| **Category** | 🟤 Basic Science |
| **PMID** | [41995870](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41995870/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1007/s00285-026-02382-6](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00285-026-02382-6) |

**Summary:** The brain represents a critical HIV reservoir that poses unique therapeutic challenges due to blood-brain barrier (BBB) permeability and regional heterogeneity. This mathematical modelling study developed a spatiotemporal model of HIV infection across three brain regions (prefrontal cortex, choroid plexus, primary visual cortex) to assess how drug pharmacodynamics and CNS Penetration-Effectiveness (CPE) scores influence viral control. The model identifies an "infection invasion threshold" that can be used to select drug regimens with pharmacodynamic properties (dose, half-life, dosing interval, CPE score) capable of preventing or controlling HIV in the brain. Results highlight the importance of CPE scoring in treatment selection for HIV-associated neurocognitive disorders (HAND), providing a theoretical framework for optimizing CNS-targeted ART regimens.

## Key Themes

**1. Long-acting injectables dominate the prevention landscape.** Three of this week's publications address CAB-LA or novel long-acting PrEP formats — from PK data on genital tract penetration to testing strategies and next-generation TAF implants — reflecting the field's accelerating shift away from daily oral PrEP toward long-acting options.

**2. Two-drug ART continues to expand its evidence base.** The DTG/3TC analysis in patients with prior HBV exposure reinforces that simplified two-drug regimens can match three-drug efficacy even in populations previously thought to require broader coverage, reducing pill burden and long-term toxicity.

**3. Global equity gaps in treatment outcomes remain urgent.** The African meta-analysis (81% VS in pregnant women vs. a 95% target) and the ADAP implementation protocol both highlight that achieving treatment goals requires systems-level solutions — not just better drugs.

**4. AI tools for ARV pharmacology are not yet trustworthy.** The ACCURATE-DDI study is a timely warning: ChatGPT failed on 60% of serious DDI pairs, underscoring that AI-generated drug interaction advice for HIV regimens must not substitute for validated specialist resources or clinical pharmacist review.

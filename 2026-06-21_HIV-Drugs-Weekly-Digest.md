## 1. Unsuppressed Viremia and Lower CD4 Count Associated With Faster Telomere Attrition in African Children With Perinatal HIV on Long-term Antiretroviral Therapy

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 16 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *The Journal of Infectious Diseases* |
| **Study type** | Tier 2 — Phase III RCT sub-analysis (VITALITY trial) |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment · 🟤 Basic Science |
| **Source** | via PubMed |
| **PMID** | [41655979](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41655979/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1093/infdis/jiag060](https://doi.org/10.1093/infdis/jiag060) |

**Summary:** This analysis of 842 adolescents aged 11–19 with perinatal HIV on cART in Zambia and Zimbabwe (VITALITY trial) found that VL >1,000 copies/mL was independently associated with shorter telomere length (β = −0.239, 95% CI −0.451 to −0.026; P = 0.028) and accelerated telomere attrition over 48 weeks compared with those with VL <1,000 copies/mL. Lower CD4 count was also associated with faster attrition (β = −0.033 per 100 CD4 cells/µL; P = 0.009). These findings demonstrate that persistent viremia causes measurable immune aging within one year, reinforcing the urgency of achieving and sustaining viral suppression in pediatric ART programs in sub-Saharan Africa.

## 2. Long-Acting Cabotegravir/Rilpivirine Reduces Immune-Activation and -Senescence in People With HIV With CMV Co-Infection

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 16 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Immunology* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Prospective comparative cohort |
| **Category** | 🟣 Pharmacology · 🔵 Treatment |
| **Source** | via PubMed |
| **PMID** | [42300985](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42300985/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1111/imm.70154](https://doi.org/10.1111/imm.70154) |

**Summary:** In a prospective Italian cohort of 37 people with HIV (PWH) switching to long-acting injectable CAB/RPV compared with 9 continuing oral ART, switching was associated with significant reductions in T-cell immune activation (CD4 and CD8 activation lower at weeks 48 and 72) and immune senescence (CD8 senescence reduced at weeks 28, 48, and 72). Delta analysis confirmed greater immunological benefits with LA therapy versus oral ART. CMV-specific T-cell responses transiently increased at week 28 before returning to baseline, and CMV remained a key driver of residual immune dysfunction. These results suggest that LA formulations may confer immunological benefits beyond viral suppression, offering improved immune homeostasis in people with CMV co-infection.

## 3. High Frequency of HIV-1 Drug Resistance Among Individuals With Persistent Low-Level Viremia During First-Line Antiretroviral Therapy in Uganda

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 16 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *BMC Infectious Diseases* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Cross-sectional study |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **Source** | via PubMed |
| **PMID** | [42304252](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42304252/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1186/s12879-026-13774-w](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12879-026-13774-w) |

**Summary:** Among 444 Ugandan adults on first-line ART with persistent low-level viremia (PLLV, two consecutive VL results <1,000 copies/mL) and adherence ≥95%, 62.7% of successfully genotyped individuals harboured drug resistance mutations (DRMs). NNRTI mutations were most common (56.7%), including K103N (28.4%) and G190A (23.9%); M184V was the dominant NRTI DRM (35.8%). VL between 500–999 copies/mL was strongly associated with resistance (aOR 6.4, 95% CI 1.49–27.89). These findings challenge the assumption that PLLV with good adherence is virologically benign, and support routine genotyping for individuals in this range — including adoption of next-generation sequencing platforms to overcome low genotyping success rates at low VL levels.

## 4. Trends and Spatial Distribution of Low-Level Viremia Among Ugandan Children and Adolescents, 2014–2023

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 18 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *PLoS ONE* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Retrospective national cohort |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment · ⚪ Epidemiology |
| **Source** | via PubMed |
| **PMID** | [42313809](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42313809/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1371/journal.pone.0339549](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0339549) |

**Summary:** Analysis of 974,873 viral load tests from Uganda's Laboratory Information Management System for children and adolescents aged 0–19 from 2014–2023 found LLV prevalence rose from 10.5% to 16.2% nationally (p < 0.001), with the steepest increase among infants (12.7% to 22.5%). By 2023, five of fifteen regions recorded LLV prevalence above 20%. Increases were observed across first-line regimens, long-duration ART users, and those with reportedly good adherence. Taken together with the week's findings on drug resistance in PLLV (see article 3 above), this epidemiological trend signals a growing virological risk in Uganda's pediatric HIV program that demands enhanced VL monitoring and tailored response.

## 5. A Precautionary Tale of Lenacapavir Drug-Drug Interactions: A Case Series of Two Persons Living With HIV Receiving Long-Acting Injections

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 18 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *International Journal of STD & AIDS* |
| **Study type** | Tier 5 — Case series |
| **Category** | 🟣 Pharmacology |
| **Source** | via PubMed |
| **PMID** | [42312466](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42312466/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1177/09564624261461789](https://doi.org/10.1177/09564624261461789) |

**Summary:** Two cases of clinically significant drug-drug interactions with ambulatory subcutaneous lenacapavir are reported from Virginia Commonwealth University Health. Case 1 involved a potential interaction with clopidogrel, discontinued without adverse outcome. Case 2 involved lenacapavir combined with inhaled and nasal fluticasone producing symptoms consistent with adrenal insufficiency, with low baseline cortisol confirmed and improvement following fluticasone discontinuation. The authors updated institutional EMR alerting systems to flag lenacapavir DDIs similarly to those for ritonavir and cobicistat. As lenacapavir is approved both for highly treatment-experienced PLWH and for PrEP, this case series raises important pharmacovigilance considerations for prescribers at the point of initiation and during co-medication review.

## 6. Long-Acting Injectable Cabotegravir/Rilpivirine in a Pregnant Woman Living With HIV With Hyperemesis Gravidarum: A Case Report and Review of Literature

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 17 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *International Journal of STD & AIDS* |
| **Study type** | Tier 5 — Case report and literature review |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **Source** | via PubMed |
| **PMID** | [42306935](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42306935/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1177/09564624261461198](https://doi.org/10.1177/09564624261461198) |

**Summary:** A 26-year-old woman living with HIV-1 (CRF02_AG subtype) in Italy with a history of poor adherence experienced virologic rebound to 57,950 copies/mL at gestational week 28, attributed to severe hyperemesis gravidarum (HEG) impairing oral drug absorption. With no resistance mutations detected, CAB/RPV LA (600/900 mg IM) was initiated at GW29; viral suppression (<20 copies/mL) was achieved within four weeks. An elective caesarean section at GW36 resulted in an HIV-negative neonate with no adverse maternal or neonatal outcomes. This case highlights HEG as an under-recognised mechanism of ART failure in pregnancy and supports CAB/RPV LA as a viable rescue strategy — while emphasising the urgent need for prospective pharmacokinetic and safety data in pregnant people.

## 7. A Screen of Chromatin-Targeting Compounds Identifies TAF1 as a Novel Regulator of HIV Latency

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 15 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *mBio* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — In vitro screen with CRISPR/Cas9 functional validation |
| **Category** | 🟤 Basic Science |
| **Source** | via PubMed |
| **PMID** | [42294674](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42294674/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1128/mbio.01183-26](https://doi.org/10.1128/mbio.01183-26) |

**Summary:** Researchers at UNC Chapel Hill screened 84 chromatin-targeting small molecules and identified BAY-299 — a bromodomain inhibitor selective for TAF1 and BRD1 — as an HIV latency-modulating compound. CRISPR/Cas9 knockout demonstrated that TAF1 (but not BRD1) is essential for maintaining HIV latency and that TAF1 depletion selectively increases HIV transcription with minimal host gene effects. TAF1 depletion also increased histone acetylation at the viral promoter, suggesting a chromatin-based mechanism. BAY-299 enhanced the efficacy of established latency-reversing agents (vorinostat, prostratin, iBET-151). TAF1 represents a novel and potentially selective epigenetic target for HIV cure strategies, adding to the growing inventory of host factors that regulate the latent reservoir.

## 8. Assessing Overall PrEP Uptake, Daily PrEP and Event-Driven PrEP Use Among Men Who Have Sex With Men in 15 Countries Across Asia

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 15 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Sexual Health* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Cross-sectional online survey |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention |
| **Source** | via PubMed |
| **PMID** | [42219253](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42219253/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1071/SH25064](https://doi.org/10.1071/SH25064) |

**Summary:** The PrEP APPEAL study surveyed 15,012 cisgender MSM across 15 Asian countries and found that while 80.3% were PrEP-aware, only 17.9% were current users (11.6% daily PrEP, 6.3% event-driven [ED-PrEP]). ED-PrEP use was highest in Laos (73.9%), Taiwan (70.2%), and Japan (57.1%), while overall uptake was low in lower-income settings. Factors independently associated with non-use (vs ED-PrEP) included bisexual identity and condomless vaginal sex; factors associated with daily PrEP included being in a relationship and recent STI testing. Promoting choice between dosing regimens and targeted outreach are needed to address substantial prevention gaps across the region.

## 9. Disparities in PrEP Use Among HIV-Negative Black and Latina Women of Transgender Experience and Transfeminine People in Los Angeles County, 2019–2020 to 2023–2024

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 17 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *AIDS and Behavior* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Cross-sectional surveillance (two time-points) |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention · 🟡 Implementation |
| **Source** | via PubMed |
| **PMID** | [42307861](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42307861/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1007/s10461-026-05208-0](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10461-026-05208-0) |

**Summary:** Surveillance of Black and Latina women of transgender experience and transfeminine people (WTE/TFP) in LA County showed diverging PrEP trajectories: Latina uptake rose from 28% to 49%, while Black participant uptake fell from 32% to 21% between 2019–20 and 2023–24. Hormone use and methamphetamine use were associated with higher PrEP uptake; access to trusted gender-affirming providers increased PrEP use more for Black than Latina participants. These findings highlight worsening HIV prevention disparities for Black transgender women and underscore the need for community-led approaches and integration of HIV prevention within gender-affirming care settings.

## 10. Early Implementation of Doxycycline Post-Exposure Prophylaxis at an Infectious Diseases Clinic in the Southern United States

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 16 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *AIDS Patient Care and STDs* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Retrospective cohort |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention · 🟡 Implementation |
| **Source** | via PubMed |
| **PMID** | [42304728](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42304728/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1177/10872914261461631](https://doi.org/10.1177/10872914261461631) |

**Summary:** A retrospective analysis of DoxyPEP prescribing at a university-affiliated HIV/PrEP clinic in Durham, North Carolina (through December 2024) enrolled 219 DoxyPEP recipients and found that PrEP users were disproportionately represented (53% of recipients vs. 90% of the clinic HIV-positive population). Among 276 MSM/TGW with a bacterial STI during the study period, only 26% received DoxyPEP, with PrEP use (aOR 11.1) and encounters after CDC guideline release (aOR 2.46) as the strongest predictors of receipt. Recurrent bacterial STI incidence before DoxyPEP was 51 per 100 person-years. Missed prescribing opportunities — particularly among PLWH — signal the need for systematic integration of DoxyPEP into routine HIV care protocols.

## Key Themes

**1. Long-acting injectables are expanding in scope but require rigorous pharmacovigilance.** Three articles this week — immunological benefits of CAB/RPV LA, a DDI case series with lenacapavir, and a pregnancy case report — collectively illustrate that as long-acting ARVs move into more diverse clinical contexts (virologically complex patients, pregnancy, co-medications), their risk profile demands proactive institutional safeguards including EMR alerting and prospective pharmacokinetic studies.

**2. Low-level viremia is a growing early-warning signal in sub-Saharan Africa.** Two Uganda-focused studies document rising LLV in children and adolescents and high rates of drug resistance even with reportedly good adherence at VL 500–999 copies/mL. Together, they point to critical gaps in genotyping access and the need to treat PLLV as a sentinel event rather than a monitoring artifact.

**3. PrEP delivery is uneven across geography, gender identity, and socioeconomic context.** From Asia-wide coverage gaps in MSM populations to declining uptake among Black transgender women in Los Angeles, this week's prevention literature highlights that biomedical effectiveness does not automatically translate into equitable access. Gender-affirming care integration and dosing-regimen flexibility emerge as key levers.

**4. The HIV cure field is identifying new epigenetic targets with selective profiles.** The identification of TAF1 as an HIV latency regulator — with host-gene-sparing selectivity — adds momentum to a growing class of chromatin-targeting latency reversal strategies. This builds on established work with HDAC inhibitors and BET bromodomain inhibitors, potentially enabling more precise "shock-and-kill" combinations.

## 1. Safety and Efficacy of Switching to Bictegravir–Lenacapavir (ARTISTRY-2 Phase 3 Trial)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 06 May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *The Lancet HIV* |
| **Study type** | Tier 2 — Phase 3 double-blind randomised controlled non-inferiority trial |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [42105781](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42105781/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1016/S2352-3018(26)00078-0](https://doi.org/10.1016/S2352-3018(26)00078-0) |

**Summary:** ARTISTRY-2 enrolled 577 virologically suppressed adults with HIV-1 from 100 sites across 14 countries (March–October 2024) and randomly assigned participants 2:1 to switch to oral bictegravir–lenacapavir (75–50 mg once daily) or to continue bictegravir–emtricitabine–tenofovir alafenamide (B/F/TAF) for 48 weeks. The primary endpoint — HIV-1 RNA ≥50 copies/mL at week 48 — occurred in 1.3% of the BIC-LEN arm vs 1.0% in the B/F/TAF arm (difference 0.3%, 95.002% CI −1.9 to 2.4), meeting non-inferiority at the 4% margin. Drug-related adverse events were similar between arms (10% vs 12%), with no treatment-related serious adverse events. The trial establishes bictegravir–lenacapavir as a viable new single-tablet regimen option that eliminates tenofovir and emtricitabine, potentially benefiting patients with renal or bone concerns associated with nucleoside backbone use.

## 2. VRC01 Broadly Neutralising Antibody with ART Initiation in HIV-Positive Infants

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 06 May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Science Translational Medicine* |
| **Study type** | Tier 2 — Randomised open-label two-arm trial |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [42090482](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42090482/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1126/scitranslmed.adr4509](https://doi.org/10.1126/scitranslmed.adr4509) |

**Summary:** This IMPAACT network trial randomised 61 infants with HIV-1 (within 14 days of ART initiation) to receive subcutaneous VRC01 (40 mg/kg at weeks 0, 2, 6, and 10) or no VRC01. VRC01 was well tolerated with no safety signals. Overall decreases in HIV-1 DNA at week 14 did not significantly differ between arms, though post-hoc analyses showed higher VRC01 plasma concentrations correlated with greater HIV-1 DNA decline — suggesting a concentration-dependent effect that was limited by lower-than-predicted trough levels. Baseline resistance to VRC01 was detected in a subset of infants. The findings support safety of broadly neutralising antibodies (bNAbs) in infants and underscore the need for more potent bNAb candidates or combination approaches to achieve reservoir reduction during early treatment.

## 3. Long-Acting Injectable ART: Promise for Adherence, Persistent Gaps in Global Access (LATITUDE Commentary)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 08 May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Med (New York, N.Y.)* |
| **Study type** | Tier 5 — Expert commentary / opinion |
| **Category** | 📄 Review/Opinion · 🟡 Implementation |
| **PMID** | [42105739](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42105739/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1016/j.medj.2026.101137](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.medj.2026.101137) |

**Summary:** Authors from the Desmond Tutu HIV Centre (University of Cape Town) comment on the LATITUDE trial, which demonstrated superiority of monthly injectable cabotegravir + rilpivirine over standard oral ART in people with HIV who have adherence challenges. The commentary highlights that while long-acting formulations offer transformative potential for adherence-challenged populations, persistent barriers to global access — including high costs, cold-chain requirements, and healthcare infrastructure needs — risk limiting benefits to high-income settings. The piece calls for equity-centred implementation strategies as these formulations scale, particularly for sub-Saharan African contexts where adherence barriers are most acute.

## 4. Factors Associated with Immunological Non-Response in HIV Patients on ART in Southeast Asia — Systematic Review

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 09 May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Antiviral Therapy* |
| **Study type** | Tier 1 — Systematic review |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [42104775](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42104775/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1177/13596535261450699](https://doi.org/10.1177/13596535261450699) |

**Summary:** This systematic review synthesised data from 10 observational studies (n = 6,988) published 2010–2025 examining immunological non-response (INR) — defined as failure to achieve adequate CD4 recovery despite virological suppression — in people with HIV on ART in Southeast Asia. Baseline CD4 count at ART initiation emerged as the most consistent predictor: lower baseline CD4 was robustly associated with INR even when viral loads were fully suppressed. Other significant factors included advanced age, male sex, elevated HIV RNA at initiation, and co-infections. The review underlines the critical role of early HIV diagnosis and prompt ART initiation in preventing long-term immune damage and reinforces the public health imperative of achieving the first 95 target in the region.

## 5. Weight Gain after Switch to Dolutegravir in a Large Ugandan Cohort

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 07 May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *AIDS* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Retrospective cohort |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [42097858](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42097858/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1097/QAD.0000000000004540](https://doi.org/10.1097/QAD.0000000000004540) |

**Summary:** This retrospective analysis of 5,489 virologically suppressed adults (59.6% female) at the Infectious Diseases Institute in Kampala quantified weight gain after switching ART anchor drugs to dolutegravir (DTG). The rate of gain was 0.12 kg/month in the first year, with a cumulative ≥10% weight gain risk of 37.8% by 36 months. Female sex, age 25–49 years, and prior efavirenz or protease inhibitor use were independent risk factors; higher pre-switch BMI was protective. Overall, 15.7% would meet proposed expert criteria for switching away from DTG due to metabolic concerns. These findings add to the growing evidence base on DTG-associated weight gain in real-world sub-Saharan African settings and have implications for the sizeable proportion of patients now maintained on DTG-based regimens as the global backbone of first-line treatment.

## 6. Real-World Outcomes of Two-Drug Regimen (Dolutegravir + Lamivudine) in HIV-Suppressed Adults — 48 Weeks and Beyond

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 04 May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *HIV Medicine* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Retrospective cohort |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [42082362](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42082362/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1111/hiv.70247](https://doi.org/10.1111/hiv.70247) |

**Summary:** A Chinese-majority retrospective cohort (n = 352; 84.7% male; 48.7% aged ≥50 years) from Queen Elizabeth Hospital, Hong Kong, evaluated outcomes at 48 weeks after switching from standard three-drug ART to DTG + lamivudine (3TC). Virological failure occurred in <2% across both age groups with no significant difference between younger (<50) and older (≥50) patients in failure or discontinuation rates. Adults ≥50 years showed greater total cholesterol reduction post-switch (−4.8% vs +0.4%, p = 0.005). The results support the real-world durability and metabolic benefits of the two-drug regimen in an ageing HIV population, with implications for reducing long-term drug toxicity burden.

## 7. Developing a PrEP Pregnancy Surveillance System in Malawi (PrIMO/PRISM Registry)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 08 May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *BMC Pregnancy and Childbirth* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Implementation/surveillance report |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention · 🟡 Implementation |
| **PMID** | [42098649](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42098649/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1186/s12884-026-09190-2](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12884-026-09190-2) |

**Summary:** Malawi introduced long-acting injectable cabotegravir (CAB-LA) as PrEP in March 2024, but safety data in pregnancy remain limited given the historical exclusion of pregnant women from early trials. This paper describes the design and initial rollout of the PrEP Pregnancy Registry — a prospective pharmacovigilance system tracking real-world PrEP safety (oral TDF/FTC, CAB-LA, and lenacapavir) during pregnancy. Starting at 5 facilities and expanding to 35, the registry uses ScanForm AI-powered optical character recognition to digitise handwritten records and applies WHO harmonised pharmacovigilance indicators for maternal and infant outcomes. The registry model offers a scalable template for generating urgently needed real-world safety data for emerging long-acting PrEP regimens in pregnancy, particularly in resource-constrained settings where these drugs are being deployed ahead of full safety characterisation.

## 8. Bictegravir Pharmacokinetics During Pregnancy: Mechanisms of Lower Exposure and Placental Transfer

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 05 May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Journal of Antimicrobial Chemotherapy* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Open-label PK study |
| **Category** | 🟣 Pharmacology |
| **PMID** | [42090286](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42090286/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1093/jac/dkag159](https://doi.org/10.1093/jac/dkag159) |

**Summary:** This 16-participant open-label PK study (women with HIV on B/F/TAF) measured bictegravir plasma exposure in the third trimester versus 4–6 weeks postpartum. Bictegravir AUC was 51% lower during pregnancy (geometric mean ratio 0.49, 90% CI 0.44–0.55), primarily driven by increased glucuronidation (M15 metabolite 174% higher) and sulfation (M20 200% higher), as well as altered protein binding. Despite reduced systemic exposure, trough concentrations remained above the PA-IC95, no vertical transmission occurred, and no congenital anomalies were reported. Cord blood–to–maternal plasma ratio was 1.3, indicating substantial placental transfer. These findings are clinically reassuring for practitioners prescribing B/F/TAF in pregnancy, and the mechanistic data (enhanced Phase II metabolism) advance understanding of how physiological changes in pregnancy affect integrase inhibitor exposure.

## 9. Topical Vaginal/Rectal Inserts Combining Doxycycline, Tenofovir Alafenamide, and Elvitegravir for STI and HIV Prevention

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 06 May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *AAPS PharmSciTech* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Pharmaceutical development / in vitro characterisation |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention · 🟣 Pharmacology |
| **PMID** | [42091755](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42091755/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1208/s12249-026-03427-1](https://doi.org/10.1208/s12249-026-03427-1) |

**Summary:** Researchers at CONRAD (Old Dominion University) and Philadelphia College of Pharmacy developed two on-demand topical insert formulations: a doxycycline (DOX) insert for bacterial STI prevention (10–100 mg) and a multipurpose TED insert combining DOX (10 mg), tenofovir alafenamide (20 mg), and elvitegravir (16 mg) to simultaneously target bacterial STIs and HIV. In vitro, both insert types disintegrated within 10 minutes with >95% drug release within 60 minutes, met key physicochemical targets, and showed stability projecting a shelf life exceeding 2 years (DOX inserts). The TED insert represents a first-in-class multipurpose rectal/vaginal prevention product, offering an event-driven alternative to long-term systemic PrEP for populations who prefer on-demand options — an important complement to the growing long-acting systemic PrEP pipeline.

## 10. Lenacapavir Capsid Inhibitor Analogue Does Not Affect Estrous Cyclicity in Mice

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 09 May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Journal of Neuroimmune Pharmacology* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Preclinical (in vivo, murine) |
| **Category** | 🟤 Basic Science · 🟢 Prevention |
| **PMID** | [42105019](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42105019/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1007/s11481-026-10293-y](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11481-026-10293-y) |

**Summary:** Concerns about PrEP impacts on the menstrual cycle are a documented barrier to uptake among women and adolescents. This Drexel University preclinical study administered CA inhibitor 1 — an analogue of lenacapavir — to adult female C57BL/6J mice and assessed estrous cyclicity, astrocyte immunoreactivity, and activity in the medial preoptic area (mPOA), a key regulator of the estrous cycle. Neither estrous cycle regularity nor mPOA cellular activity was altered by the injection. While animal-to-human translation requires caution, these findings are reassuring and provide preclinical support for clinical investigations of lenacapavir's menstrual effects — relevant given the drug's rapid global PrEP scale-up and the importance of addressing perceived hormonal side-effects to drive uptake in young women.

## Key Themes

**1. The long-acting injectable era is maturing — but equity remains unresolved.** This week's literature spans the full arc from new trial evidence (ARTISTRY-2, LATITUDE commentary) to real-world implementation challenges (Malawi PrEP registry) to preclinical safety work (lenacapavir and the menstrual cycle). While efficacy data for long-acting formulations are robust, multiple publications this week signal that access barriers — cost, cold chain, infrastructure — risk confining benefits to well-resourced settings.

**2. Dolutegravir dominance comes with metabolic trade-offs at scale.** Two independent real-world cohorts this week — from Uganda and Hong Kong — illuminate different facets of DTG-based regimen use: significant weight gain in African women (37.8% with ≥10% gain by 36 months in Uganda) alongside metabolic benefits of the two-drug DTG+3TC switch in older patients in Hong Kong. Together these papers push the field toward more individualised regimen selection beyond virological efficacy alone.

**3. Pregnancy is an emerging frontier for HIV pharmacology.** The bictegravir PK pregnancy study and the Malawi PrEP pregnancy registry both address a historically underserved population. The mechanistic elucidation of bictegravir's reduced exposure during pregnancy (enhanced glucuronidation/sulfation), combined with adequate trough levels and no vertical transmission, provides crucial reassurance. Meanwhile, Malawi's registry demonstrates how real-world pharmacovigilance can be operationalised rapidly as new regimens (CAB-LA, lenacapavir) are deployed in pregnant populations ahead of trial data.

**4. Novel modalities — bNAbs, multipurpose topicals, two-drug regimens — are diversifying the HIV toolkit.** The VRC01 infant trial, the TED multipurpose topical insert, and the ARTISTRY-2 nucleoside-sparing regimen each represent distinct approaches to expanding HIV treatment and prevention options. While none are yet ready for routine deployment, they collectively signal a pipeline that is moving beyond incremental optimisation of existing drug classes toward genuinely new mechanisms and delivery strategies.

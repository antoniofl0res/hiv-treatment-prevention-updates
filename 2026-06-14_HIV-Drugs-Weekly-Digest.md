## 1. Proof-of-Concept Phase 2a Trial of VH4524184 (VH-184), an Emerging Third-Generation Integrase Strand Transfer Inhibitor With an Enhanced Resistance Profile

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 12 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Clinical Infectious Diseases* |
| **Study type** | Tier 2: RCT (randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 2a monotherapy) |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **Source** | via PubMed / Europe PMC |
| **PMID** | [42283218](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42283218/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1093/cid/ciag357](https://doi.org/10.1093/cid/ciag357) |

**Summary:** This randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 2a trial tested short-course oral monotherapy with VH-184 — a third-generation INSTI being developed as a long-acting agent — in 22 ART-naïve adults with HIV-1 RNA ≥3000 copies/mL (19 active, 3 placebo). Over 10 days, mean maximum reductions in HIV-1 RNA reached −1.17, −2.15, and −2.31 log₁₀ copies/mL at the 10, 50, and 300 mg doses, respectively. No withdrawals, serious adverse events, or treatment-emergent resistance occurred through Day 10. The rapid, dose-dependent antiviral activity and clean safety profile support advancing VH-184 as a core agent in future complete (potentially long-acting) regimens with an improved barrier to integrase resistance.

## 2. Guidance on the Clinical Management of HIV-1 Persistent Low-Level Viraemia on Antiretroviral Treatment: a Scoping Review and an International Delphi Consensus

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 11 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *The Lancet HIV* |
| **Study type** | Tier 1: Scoping review + modified Delphi consensus |
| **Category** | 📄 Review/Opinion · 🔵 Treatment |
| **Source** | via PubMed / Europe PMC |
| **PMID** | [42276093](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42276093/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1016/S2352-3018(26)00107-4](https://doi.org/10.1016/S2352-3018(26)00107-4) |

**Summary:** A multidisciplinary expert panel combined a scoping review with a modified Delphi process to standardize the management of persistent low-level viraemia (LLV) on ART, a condition with inconsistent definitions across settings. The evidence most commonly framed LLV as repeated viral loads of 50–1000 copies/mL, with stronger links between the 200–1000 copies/mL stratum and virological failure, resistance, and adverse outcomes than the 50–200 stratum. The panel proposed a pragmatic framework: prompt confirmation, adherence and drug-interaction review, resistance testing (especially at 200–1000 copies/mL), broader clinical evaluation, individualized regimen optimization, and prevention counselling. The guidance aims to reduce practice variability and clarify when LLV warrants clinical action across diverse health systems.

## 3. Adherence, Acceptability, Preference and Safety of Placebo Long-Acting PrEP (LA-PrEP) Injections and Implants for HIV Prevention in South African Men

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 9 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *AIDS* |
| **Study type** | Tier 2: Randomized crossover study (SAMURAI; placebo products) |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention |
| **Source** | via PubMed / Europe PMC |
| **PMID** | [42262366](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42262366/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1097/QAD.0000000000004563](https://doi.org/10.1097/QAD.0000000000004563) |

**Summary:** The SAMURAI randomized crossover study enrolled 184 cisgender men (84 MSM, 100 men who have sex only with women) in Cape Town and Johannesburg, who received placebo bimonthly gluteal injections and a six-month upper-arm implant in randomized order. Retention was 86%; more men initiated injections than implants (97.8% vs 91.3%, P=0.007), but persistence was significantly higher for implants (aRR 1.17, 95% CI 1.07–1.27). User satisfaction was high and nearly identical for both platforms (8.6 vs 8.4), and among men who tried both, preference split almost evenly (48.4% injections, 47.7% implants). The findings underline strong male interest in long-acting PrEP and the importance of offering method choice, while flagging that visit frequency and delivery familiarity shape persistence.

## 4. Effectiveness, Sex Differences, and Patient Experience in Real-World Use of Long-Acting Cabotegravir and Rilpivirine: Results from a Prospective Study

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 11 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Infectious Diseases and Therapy* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3: Prospective observational cohort |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **Source** | via PubMed / Europe PMC |
| **PMID** | [42274912](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42274912/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1007/s40121-026-01373-w](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40121-026-01373-w) |

**Summary:** This prospective cohort at Hospital Clínic of Barcelona followed 741 people with HIV who switched to every-2-month long-acting injectable cabotegravir + rilpivirine (LA-CAB/RPV), with sex-disaggregated analysis. Among the 461 reaching week 52, on-treatment effectiveness (HIV-RNA <50 copies/mL) was 96% overall, with only 8 (1.7%) confirmed virological failures and 9% discontinuations and no significant differences by sex. Proportionally fewer women than men switched (8% vs 14% of the cohort), and women reported greater medication burden and disclosure concerns. The data confirm high real-world durability of LA-CAB/RPV while reinforcing the need for sex-disaggregated reporting to surface differential experiences.

## 5. A French National Observatory of People with HIV Initiating Lenacapavir-Based Treatment After Regulatory Approval

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 10 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Antimicrobial Agents and Chemotherapy* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3: National retrospective observational study |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment · 🟣 Pharmacology |
| **Source** | via Europe PMC / PubMed |
| **PMID** | [42268209](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42268209/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1128/aac.00228-26](https://doi.org/10.1128/aac.00228-26) |

**Summary:** This French national observatory described 96 people with HIV-1 initiating lenacapavir (LEN)-based regimens after approval, most heavily treatment-experienced (61% with a genotypic susceptibility score <1). Over a median 12 months, 94% of those virologically suppressed at baseline and 64% of those viraemic at baseline achieved viral load <50 copies/mL at last follow-up; virological failure occurred in 8 participants, with emergent capsid N74D resistance in only one. Among 24 who received cabotegravir + LEN, 18 were suppressed at last visit. The real-world data confirm that LEN-based regimens deliver durable suppression with a low rate of emergent capsid resistance in salvage populations with advanced resistance.

## 6. Characterization of HIV Acquisitions During a Large Oral PrEP Implementation Study: Insights from the ImPrEP Study

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 9 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *AIDS* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3: Implementation cohort (Brazil, Mexico, Peru) |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention |
| **Source** | via PubMed / Europe PMC |
| **PMID** | [42262379](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42262379/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1097/QAD.0000000000004562](https://doi.org/10.1097/QAD.0000000000004562) |

**Summary:** This analysis of the ImPrEP oral PrEP cohort among MSM and transgender women in Brazil, Mexico, and Peru (8714 participants, 12,185 person-years) characterized HIV acquisitions on PrEP. Acute HIV prevalence at enrollment was 0.5%, and 104 participants (1.2%) were diagnosed during follow-up; only 8.6% had tenofovir levels consistent with ≥4 doses/week at diagnosis, and resistance was uncommon (1.8% K65R, 10.5% M184V/I). Younger age and concurrent STI were associated with acute infection. The findings highlight that adherence — not resistance — drove most breakthrough infections, making the case for long-acting PrEP formulations and tailored adherence support in Latin America.

## 7. Effects of Malaria Intermittent Preventive Treatment with Dihydroartemisinin-Piperaquine on Antiretroviral Drug Concentrations in African Pregnant Women Living with HIV

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 12 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Antimicrobial Agents and Chemotherapy* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3: Pharmacokinetic sub-study of an RCT (NCT03671109) |
| **Category** | 🟣 Pharmacology |
| **Source** | via PubMed / Europe PMC |
| **PMID** | [42283563](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42283563/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1128/aac.00337-26](https://doi.org/10.1128/aac.00337-26) |

**Summary:** This PK sub-study of a trial in Mozambique and Gabon measured antiretroviral concentrations in 105 pregnant women living with HIV randomized to monthly malaria IPTp with dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine (DHA-PPQ) versus placebo. End-of-pregnancy median dolutegravir concentrations were 835 ng/mL (maternal) and 1204 ng/mL (cord), with lamivudine similarly distributed, and no significant differences between the DHA-PPQ and placebo arms. Multivariate analysis found no independent associations between ARV concentrations and participant characteristics. The results support DHA-PPQ as a safe IPTp option in this population — important because sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine is contraindicated alongside cotrimoxazole — without compromising antiretroviral exposure.

## 8. Transmitted and Pretreatment Drug Resistance Among 69 HIV-1 CRFs in China: the First Systematic Analysis

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 9 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Emerging Microbes & Infections* |
| **Study type** | Tier 1: Systematic molecular-epidemiology analysis |
| **Category** | 🟤 Basic Science · 🟣 Pharmacology |
| **Source** | via PubMed / Europe PMC |
| **PMID** | [42261238](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42261238/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1080/22221751.2026.2673651](https://doi.org/10.1080/22221751.2026.2673651) |

**Summary:** This first systematic analysis characterized drug-resistance mutation profiles across all 69 HIV-1 circulating recombinant forms (CRFs) identified in China through 2025, spanning first- to fourth-generation recombinants. Several CRFs showed moderate transmitted drug resistance (5–15%) and notably elevated pretreatment drug resistance (10.8–55.1%), with V179D/E prevalent and potentially reducing NNRTI susceptibility in combination with other mutations. The work provides critical surveillance baseline data for NNRTI selection in China and underscores the need for phenotypic validation of natural resistance mutations in novel recombinants — a pharmacologically relevant signal for first-line regimen design.

## 9. Epidemiological and Economic Effect of Preference-Driven HIV PrEP Implementation for MSM and Transgender Women Across 16 Countries and Territories in the Asia-Pacific Region: a Modelling Study

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 12 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *The Lancet HIV* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4: Mathematical modelling study |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention · ⚪ Epidemiology |
| **Source** | via PubMed / Europe PMC |
| **PMID** | [42285127](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42285127/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1016/S2352-3018(26)00053-6](https://doi.org/10.1016/S2352-3018(26)00053-6) |

**Summary:** This modelling study translated discrete-choice-experiment preference data from 16 Asia-Pacific countries and territories into projected PrEP coverage and impact for daily oral, on-demand, and long-acting injectable modalities among MSM and transgender women. Willingness to use PrEP ranged from 46.8% to 95.2% (coverage 10.8–30.8%), with long-acting injectable PrEP yielding the largest reductions in annual HIV acquisitions (17.1–31.8%) but the highest cost per acquisition averted, while on-demand PrEP was the most economically efficient. Cost-effectiveness improved sharply once incidence exceeded a critical turning point (0.22–3.22 per 100 person-years). The analysis argues for preference-driven, incidence-targeted PrEP mixes to maximize impact per dollar.

## 11. Interest in Long-Acting Injectable Antiretroviral Therapy (ART) Among Adults Living with HIV Newly Starting or Restarting ART in Cape Town, South Africa

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 11 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *AIDS Care* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4: Cross-sectional survey with qualitative analysis |
| **Category** | 🟡 Implementation · 🔵 Treatment |
| **Source** | via PubMed / Europe PMC |
| **PMID** | [42281163](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42281163/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1080/09540121.2026.2682339](https://doi.org/10.1080/09540121.2026.2682339) |

**Summary:** This cross-sectional survey at two peri-urban Cape Town clinics assessed interest in long-acting injectable ART (LAI ART) among 329 adults newly starting or restarting treatment — a group currently outside eligibility, which is limited to virologically suppressed patients. Some 74% were "very interested," 15% somewhat interested/unsure, and only 11% uninterested, with participants valuing freedom from daily pills, reduced adherence stress, and privacy; concerns centered on injection fear and side effects. The strong demand among unstable/restarting patients suggests that expanding LAI ART eligibility, once feasible, could advance individual and population treatment goals. As a hypothetical-preference survey, it gauges acceptability rather than clinical outcomes.

## Key Themes

**Long-acting modalities dominate both treatment and prevention pipelines.** From a third-generation INSTI in early trials (VH-184) and real-world LA-CAB/RPV durability, to lenacapavir salvage data, placebo injection/implant acceptability, and same-day injectable PrEP delivery, the week's evidence converges on injectables and implants as the central direction of travel — with the field now moving from efficacy questions to delivery, persistence, and eligibility expansion.

**Adherence, not resistance, is the recurring failure mode in prevention.** The ImPrEP analysis showed most oral-PrEP breakthrough infections occurred at sub-protective drug levels rather than through resistance, directly motivating the long-acting formulations explored elsewhere in this digest. Resistance surveillance remains important on the treatment side (China CRF analysis; low capsid resistance with lenacapavir), but the prevention bottleneck is sustained exposure.

**Choice and preference are emerging as formal implementation levers.** The Asia-Pacific modelling study, the SAMURAI near-even split between injections and implants, and high stated interest in LAI ART in Cape Town all treat user preference as a quantifiable input to coverage and cost-effectiveness — signalling a shift from one-size-fits-all to preference-matched, incidence-targeted programming.

**Equity and disaggregated data run through the set.** Sex differences in LA-CAB/RPV uptake and experience, gendered disclosure concerns, salvage outcomes in heavily treatment-experienced patients, malaria–ART co-treatment safety in pregnancy, and rapid-access models for underserved Southern US populations collectively emphasize that the next gains depend on reaching groups historically underserved by standard regimens.

## 1. Efavirenz 400 mg vs. 600 mg Combined with Lamivudine and Tenofovir in Treatment-Naïve HIV-Infected Patients in China: A Randomized, Multi-Centered, Controlled Trial

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 2 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *International Journal of Infectious Diseases* |
| **Study type** | Tier 2 — Randomized controlled trial (multicenter) |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [42235914](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42235914/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1016/j.ijid.2026.108859](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijid.2026.108859) |

**Summary:** This multicenter RCT randomized 422 ART-naïve Chinese adults with HIV-1 to efavirenz 400 mg versus 600 mg daily, each with lamivudine and tenofovir, with virological suppression (HIV-RNA <50 copies/mL) at week 72 as the primary endpoint. Suppression was achieved in 86.5% (400 mg) versus 84.1% (600 mg) — a difference of +2.5 points (95% CI −4.3 to 9.2), meeting non-inferiority. The 600 mg arm had significantly more serious non-rash adverse events and a numerically higher discontinuation rate. The findings support dose reduction to EFV 400 mg for improved tolerability without loss of efficacy in this population.

## 2. People with HIV Continuing on Complex Regimens in the Era of Treatment Optimization: Characteristics and Outcomes

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 4 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *AIDS Patient Care and STDs* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Retrospective cohort |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [42244221](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42244221/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1177/10872914261455402](https://doi.org/10.1177/10872914261455402) |

**Summary:** Using EMR data from the US Trio Health HIV Network plus Labcorp genotypes, this retrospective cohort characterized virologically suppressed people with HIV who remain on complex antiretroviral regimens. Of 43,236 people with ARV prescriptions, 3,320 (8%) were suppressed on complex regimens (69% multi-tablet, 11% with two-class resistance; median age 52, 77% male). Over a median 2.2 years of follow-up, only 4% experienced virologic failure. The persistence of this 8% subgroup — often driven by resistance, interactions, or contraindications — underscores the need for clinician education and novel switch options as long-acting and salvage agents expand.

## 3. Central Nervous System HIV Persistence and Clinical Consequences During Suppressive ART

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 1 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Current HIV/AIDS Reports* |
| **Study type** | 📄 Review (narrative) |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [42223553](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42223553/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1007/s11904-026-00786-z](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11904-026-00786-z) |

**Summary:** This review synthesizes recent evidence on how HIV invades and persists within CNS macrophages and microglia despite long-term suppressive ART, supporting the existence of a CNS reservoir. It covers neurosymptomatic CSF HIV-RNA escape, CNS-specific resistance, and ART optimization, and recommends that cognitive evaluation in people with HIV include comprehensive neurologic workup and cessation of efavirenz. Cure strategies — shock-and-kill, block-and-lock, and broadly neutralizing antibodies — must account for CNS reservoir dynamics and potential neurotoxicity. The CNS reservoir is framed as a clinically consequential barrier to cure.

## 4. Injectable Long-Acting Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis for HIV Prevention: Advancements and Future Directions in Pharmacotherapy

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 6 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Expert Opinion on Pharmacotherapy* |
| **Study type** | 📄 Review (narrative) |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention |
| **PMID** | [42240197](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42240197/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1080/14656566.2026.2685830](https://doi.org/10.1080/14656566.2026.2685830) |

**Summary:** This clinically focused narrative review summarizes intramuscular cabotegravir-LA (bi-monthly) and subcutaneous lenacapavir (twice-yearly) for PrEP, drawing on pivotal RCTs, pharmacology, safety, resistance and HIV-testing considerations, and early implementation data through February 2026. Both agents are highly efficacious, but scale-up depends on simplified testing pathways, reliable supply, acceptability, and low-burden delivery models. The authors argue that semiannual — and potentially annual — dosing could shift PrEP from "medication adherence" to "service access," improving equity if procurement and implementation barriers are addressed.

## 5. Survival and Risk Factors of Mortality Among Patients with TB/HIV Coinfection in Kigali City, Rwanda: A Retrospective Cohort Study (2019–2024)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 5 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *BMC Infectious Diseases* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Retrospective cohort |
| **Category** | 🟡 Implementation |
| **PMID** | [42249314](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42249314/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1186/s12879-026-13744-2](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12879-026-13744-2) |

**Summary:** This retrospective cohort followed 1,966 TB/HIV co-infected adults on ART across 48 Kigali health facilities (2019–2024). Overall 168-day survival was 86.6%, but 13.4% died — 78.8% of deaths within the first 56 days. Independent predictors of mortality included age ≥45, absence of cotrimoxazole or TB preventive therapy, comorbidities (aHR 3.18), undernutrition, fair ART adherence, and CD4 <200. The authors call for integrated TB/HIV care with adherence and nutritional support, expanded preventive therapy, and early intervention to cut early-treatment mortality.

## 6. Tracing the Journey: Patient Engagement in Long-Term Antiretroviral Therapy Care in Malawi

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 1 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *PLoS One* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Retrospective cohort |
| **Category** | 🟡 Implementation |
| **PMID** | [42224296](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42224296/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1371/journal.pone.0323983](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0323983) |

**Summary:** This cohort examined two-year ART engagement among 6,303 clients at two Lilongwe clinics, comparing ART "Buddy" peer support (with and without phone access) against a two-way texting (2wT) system. 2wT clients showed the highest continuous engagement and best 24-month retention (70%), versus 58% (Buddy with phone) and 27% (Buddy without phone). Engagement was dynamic — 31% of retained clients cycled in and out of care. Tailoring retention support to engagement patterns and time on ART could meaningfully improve long-term retention.

## 7. Change in Plasma Cryptococcal Antigen Titers in Uganda Among Outpatients with Advanced HIV Disease from 2017 to 2022 with Rollout of National Cryptococcal Screening

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 2 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *PLoS One* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Prospective cohort |
| **Category** | ⚪ Epidemiology |
| **PMID** | [42228705](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42228705/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1371/journal.pone.0349854](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0349854) |

**Summary:** This prospective screening study assessed plasma cryptococcal antigen (CrAg) titers in 436 Ugandan adults with advanced HIV disease (CD4 ≤200) from 2017 to 2022. Despite expanded national CrAg screening and ART access, median quarterly titers did not decline (median 1:80), while the proportion ART-naïve at screening rose significantly — 58% had never taken ART by 2022. The persistence of high titers and late presentation signals ongoing failures in early linkage to care and adherence, with implications for AIDS-related mortality from cryptococcal meningitis.

## 8. Spatiotemporal Patterns of AIDS-Defining and Non-AIDS-Defining Cancers Before and After Antiretroviral Therapy Rollout in Botswana, 1990 to 2021

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 2 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Cancer Epidemiology, Biomarkers & Prevention* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3/4 — Registry-based descriptive epidemiology |
| **Category** | ⚪ Epidemiology |
| **PMID** | [41874447](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41874447/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-25-0913](https://doi.org/10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-25-0913) |

**Summary:** Drawing on 27,726 cancer cases in the Botswana National Cancer Registry (1990–2021), this Bayesian spatial analysis mapped AIDS-defining (ADC) and non-AIDS-defining cancers (NADC) across ART expansion. Nearly half (49.5%) of cases occurred among people living with HIV, who had higher incidence of ADCs, NADCs, cervical cancer, and Kaposi sarcoma than HIV-negative individuals. Age-standardized incidence and mortality rose across districts during ART scale-up, concentrated in urbanized regions. The findings argue for integrating cancer screening and treatment into HIV care as the population ages on ART.

## 9. Population Pharmacokinetics of Lumefantrine in Pregnant and Non-Pregnant Nigerian Women Receiving Efavirenz-Based Antiretroviral Therapy

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 3 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *European Journal of Clinical Pharmacology* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Population PK / pharmacometric study |
| **Category** | 🟣 Pharmacology |
| **PMID** | [42234159](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42234159/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1007/s00228-026-04090-y](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00228-026-04090-y) |

**Summary:** This population PK study modeled lumefantrine (antimalarial partner drug) exposure in pregnant and non-pregnant Nigerian women on efavirenz-based ART, using nonlinear mixed-effects modeling and Monte Carlo simulation. A two-compartment transit-absorption model fit best; neither pregnancy nor CYP3A5 genotype significantly altered PK. Critically, only ~65% of simulated patients reached the day-7 target (0.2 µg/mL) under standard 3-day dosing, rising to ~96% with a 5-day regimen. The data support evaluating extended artemether-lumefantrine dosing in patients on EFV-based ART to avoid subtherapeutic malaria treatment.

## 10. External Evaluation of Population Pharmacokinetic Models of Cabotegravir During Oral and Intramuscular Administration in HIV-Infected Patients

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *CPT: Pharmacometrics & Systems Pharmacology* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Observational / multicenter external model validation |
| **Category** | 🟣 Pharmacology |
| **PMID** | [42170807](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42170807/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1002/psp4.70180](https://doi.org/10.1002/psp4.70180) |

**Summary:** Long-acting injectable cabotegravir shows persistent PK variability, raising interest in model-informed precision dosing (MIPD). This study externally validated two published population PK models against the French ANRS0255 CARLAPOP cohort (736 patients, 2,192 concentrations). Han's model outperformed Thoueille's (e.g. intramuscular MDAPE 17.9% vs 15.3% for IPRED), but fewer than 70% of predictions fell within a 20% error margin for either, and Thoueille's showed systematic bias in women, non-smokers, and higher-BMI patients. Neither model was deemed reliable enough for routine MIPD without further refinement — a caution for clinical PK-guided dosing of CAB-LA.

## 12. Efavirenz Mitigates Dyslipidemia and Attenuates Ulcerative Colitis Through Gut Microbiota Modulation

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 1 June 2026 |
| **Journal** | *ACS Infectious Diseases* |
| **Study type** | Tier 5 — Preclinical (in vitro / rat model) |
| **Category** | 🟤 Basic Science |
| **PMID** | [42225309](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42225309/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1021/acsinfecdis.6c00330](https://doi.org/10.1021/acsinfecdis.6c00330) |

**Summary:** This preclinical study explored how efavirenz interacts with ART-associated dyslipidemia and ulcerative colitis. In a high-fat-diet rat model, efavirenz attenuated lipid elevations and restored DHCR7 expression in DSS-induced colitis tissue, suppressing 7-dehydrocholesterol-driven Akt phosphorylation and inflammation; fecal microbiota transplant from EFV-treated rats reproduced the benefit. The work proposes a gut-microbiota-mediated mechanism by which efavirenz may modulate lipid metabolism and comorbid inflammation — hypothesis-generating for managing metabolic complications in people with HIV, pending human validation.

## Key Themes

**Optimization over novelty.** Much of this week's treatment evidence is about refining existing drugs rather than introducing new ones — efavirenz dose reduction (EFV 400 mg non-inferiority), characterizing the minority who cannot simplify to single-tablet regimens, and precision-dosing questions for long-acting cabotegravir. The field is consolidating around tolerability, simplification, and individualized dosing.

**Prevention is pivoting to long-acting injectables and access.** The standout prevention piece reframes twice-yearly lenacapavir and bi-monthly CAB-LA as a shift from "adherence" to "service access," while excluded high-impact commentary echoed the same equity-and-procurement theme around global rollout. Delivery models and supply — not efficacy — are now the rate-limiting step.

**The hardest problems are implementation, not pharmacology.** Sub-Saharan cohorts (Rwanda, Malawi, Uganda) repeatedly show that early mortality, retention gaps, late presentation, and rising ART-naïve rates drive poor outcomes despite available drugs. Two-way texting outperforming peer "Buddies" for retention is a concrete, scalable signal.

**Comorbidity and the aging ART population recur.** Rising cancer incidence in Botswana, CNS reservoir persistence, cryptococcal disease, and efavirenz–metabolism interactions all point to long-term sequelae of living with HIV on suppressive therapy as a growing clinical frontier.

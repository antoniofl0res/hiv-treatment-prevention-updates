## 1. Evaluation of Relative Bioavailability and Food Effect of a Fixed-Dose Combination Tablet of Islatravir and Lenacapavir

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Journal of Clinical Pharmacology* |
| **Study type** | Tier 2 — Phase I Clinical Trial |
| **Category** | 🟣 Pharmacology |
| **PMID** | [42053471](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42053471/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1002/jcph.70193](https://doi.org/10.1002/jcph.70193) |

**Summary:** This open-label Phase I pharmacokinetic study in 93 HIV-negative volunteers compared a fixed-dose combination (FDC) tablet of islatravir 2 mg + lenacapavir 300 mg against individual agents administered together, under fasted and fed conditions. The FDC showed comparable overall exposures (AUC) for both drugs, though islatravir peak concentration was 36% lower with the FDC under fasted conditions — a difference judged clinically acceptable given the drug's mechanism as a nucleoside reverse transcriptase translocation inhibitor. Food had no meaningful impact on islatravir pharmacokinetics, while lenacapavir exposure increased with food, consistent with established single-agent behaviour. The safety profile was consistent with known single-agent data, supporting advancement of once-weekly oral islatravir/lenacapavir as a simplified treatment option for HIV-1 without food restrictions — a key finding for ongoing Phase 3 investigations.

## 2. Incidence, Risk Factors, and Cardiovascular Impact of Hypertension in People with HIV: A Secondary Analysis of the REPRIEVE Trial

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *The Lancet HIV* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Secondary Analysis of Global RCT |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [42067280](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42067280/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1016/S2352-3018(26)00036-6](https://doi.org/10.1016/S2352-3018(26)00036-6) |

**Summary:** This prespecified secondary analysis of the REPRIEVE trial (n=4,989 participants without baseline hypertension, from a global randomised placebo-controlled trial of pitavastatin) found that 13% of people with HIV developed new hypertension over a median 5-year follow-up, at a rate of approximately 24–30 per 1,000 person-years. Pitavastatin reduced incident hypertension by 17% (cause-specific HR 0.83, 95% CI 0.71–0.97), adding a previously unrecognised benefit to its known cardiovascular effects. Incident hypertension was independently associated with a more than twofold higher risk of subsequent major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE), underscoring its clinical significance in this population. These findings strengthen the case for routine hypertension surveillance and suggest pitavastatin confers broader cardiometabolic protection than lipid-lowering alone among people with HIV on stable ART.

## 3. Real-World Adherence, Persistence, and Health Care Resource Utilization Among People with HIV Receiving Long-Acting Cabotegravir Plus Rilpivirine Compared with Oral ART: The ABOVE Study

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Journal of Managed Care & Specialty Pharmacy* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Retrospective US Cohort Study |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [42043923](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42043923/) |
| **DOI** | [10.18553/jmcp.2026.32.5.584](https://doi.org/10.18553/jmcp.2026.32.5.584) |

**Summary:** Using US administrative claims data (2020–2023), ABOVE compared 1,245 people who switched to long-acting injectable cabotegravir + rilpivirine (CAB+RPV LA) against 58,644 who remained on oral ART. CAB+RPV LA recipients showed dramatically higher 12-month adherence (73.7% vs. 30.1%, adjusted OR 8.06, 95% CI 6.62–9.81) and longer treatment persistence (median 424 vs. 393 days). Beyond virological outcomes, the LA cohort also showed higher rates of preventive care (vaccinations, cancer/STI screenings, bone density testing) and lower hospitalisation rates. These real-world data corroborate trial findings and suggest that switching to long-acting regimens may catalyse broader healthcare re-engagement — an important consideration for policy decisions around expanding LAI access.

## 4. Pharmacokinetics, Pharmacodynamics, Efficacy and Drug Resistance Selection of Injectable Long-Acting Lenacapavir Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis Against HIV

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *CPT: Pharmacometrics & Systems Pharmacology* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — PK-PD Modelling Study |
| **Category** | 🟣 Pharmacology · 🟢 Prevention |
| **PMID** | [42003411](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42003411/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1002/psp4.70254](https://doi.org/10.1002/psp4.70254) |

**Summary:** This modelling study developed an integrated PK-PD framework for twice-yearly subcutaneous lenacapavir (LEN) PrEP, incorporating pharmacokinetic variability to estimate prophylactic efficacy against wild-type virus and resistant mutants, and to quantify resistance emergence risk after discontinuation. The model estimated a fully preventive plasma concentration of ~5 ng/mL, reached within 23 hours of first injection and maintained up to 50.5 weeks post-last-dose in an average individual. Crucially, mutant selection windows for several lenacapavir resistance mutations (N74D, double mutants) overlapped with steady-state LEN concentrations, and de novo resistance could potentially emerge within 106–235 days of stopping injections — findings with important implications for discontinuation counselling and bridging strategies. These mechanistic insights are essential for designing optimal LEN-PrEP rollout protocols and managing the long pharmacological tail of this agent.

## 5. Acceptability and Preferences of Long-Acting Injectable PrEP and ART Among Men Who Have Sex With Men and People Who Inject Drugs in India

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Journal of the International AIDS Society* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Cross-Sectional Survey (Respondent-Driven Sampling) |
| **Category** | 🟢 Prevention · 🟡 Implementation |
| **PMID** | [42051162](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42051162/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1002/jia2.70114](https://doi.org/10.1002/jia2.70114) |

**Summary:** This large cross-sectional study surveyed 2,249 men who have sex with men (MSM) and 4,499 people who inject drugs (PWID) across eight Indian cities, finding high stated interest in LAI ART (89% MSM, 75% PWID) and LAI PrEP (77% MSM, 62% PWID). Despite strong acceptability of injectables, awareness of PrEP was very low (20% MSM, 5% PWID), highlighting a major educational gap. Among those interested in PrEP, monthly oral formulations were preferred over injectables by both populations, pointing to the importance of developing extended-duration oral options alongside injectable regimens. These data underscore the need for demand creation and flexible delivery models as LAI products become available in low-resource settings like India, where stigma and service fragmentation remain key barriers.

## 6. Smoldering in the Sanctuary: HIV-Associated Brain Injury in the ART Era

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 1 May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *The Journal of Clinical Investigation* |
| **Study type** | Tier 1 — Narrative Review |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment · 🟤 Basic Science |
| **PMID** | [42065243](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42065243/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1172/JCI204831](https://doi.org/10.1172/JCI204831) |

**Summary:** This high-impact review synthesises emerging evidence that HIV-associated brain injury persists in the ART era not through active viral replication, but via transcription from defective proviruses, bystander immune-mediated synaptic dysfunction, and long-lived microglial epigenetic "training." The authors highlight single-cell multiomics and liquid biopsy approaches as tools reshaping understanding of the CNS viral reservoir, and discuss the growing complexity of disentangling HIV-specific pathology from aging-related comorbidity. A key translational message is the need for CNS-targeted cure strategies — standard ART alone is insufficient to resolve neuroinflammation — and the call for human-centred studies to validate preclinical findings. This review is directly relevant to therapeutic targets for people with HIV experiencing neurocognitive challenges despite viral suppression.

## 7. Bictegravir/Emtricitabine/Tenofovir Alafenamide versus Dolutegravir + Lamivudine + Tenofovir Disoproxil Fumarate in People Living with HIV Experiencing Virologic Failure in China: A Trial Protocol

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 1 May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Trials* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Randomised Controlled Trial Protocol |
| **Category** | 🔵 Treatment |
| **PMID** | [42067921](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42067921/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1186/s13063-026-09749-6](https://doi.org/10.1186/s13063-026-09749-6) |

**Summary:** This protocol describes a 14-site, open-label, non-inferiority RCT in China (n=374) comparing BIC/FTC/TAF (single-tablet) vs. DTG+3TC+TDF (multi-tablet) in people with virologic failure on first-line NNRTI-based therapy, where drug resistance testing is often inaccessible. The primary endpoint is viral suppression (HIV-1 RNA <50 copies/mL) at week 48, with a 12% non-inferiority margin. Secondary endpoints include resistance-associated mutation emergence, adherence, and patient-reported outcomes. The rationale is compelling for resource-limited settings: NNRTI failure is common in China yet managed empirically; both BIC/FTC/TAF and DTG-based regimens have high genetic barriers to resistance. Results, when available, will provide crucial evidence for second-line sequencing in settings where genotyping is unavailable.

## 8. Scaling Up HIV Self-Testing in Africa: Insights From National Programmatic Data in Eight Countries

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Journal of the International AIDS Society* |
| **Study type** | Tier 4 — Retrospective Descriptive Analysis |
| **Category** | 🟡 Implementation · 🔴 Diagnostics |
| **PMID** | [42051152](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42051152/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1002/jia2.70120](https://doi.org/10.1002/jia2.70120) |

**Summary:** This WHO-supported analysis of national programme data from eight African countries (Kenya, Lesotho, Malawi, Mozambique, South Africa, Tanzania, Uganda, Zimbabwe; 2019–2023) found wide heterogeneity in HIVST scale-up: HIVST accounted for 63% of total testing in Lesotho but under 15% in Kenya, Tanzania, and South Africa. Despite shifting testing modalities, new HIV diagnoses and ART initiations remained broadly stable, suggesting programmes adapted effectively. COVID-19 disruptions were partially offset by HIVST in Malawi, Lesotho, and Zimbabwe. The findings affirm HIVST as a viable complement to conventional testing services and provide a baseline for monitoring progress toward the 2030 UNAIDS targets, while noting limitations in accounting for repeat testers.

## 9. Antiretroviral Therapy Blocks Natural Selection on Protective and Disease-Susceptible HLA-B Alleles in HIV-1 Infection

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 27 April 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Historical Cohort + Population Genetic Modelling |
| **Category** | 🟤 Basic Science |
| **PMID** | [42044322](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42044322/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1073/pnas.2502683123](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2502683123) |

**Summary:** Using a historical mother-child cohort from KwaZulu-Natal (1998–2025) and population genetic modelling, this PNAS study demonstrates that HIV-1 was actively reshaping HLA-B allele frequencies in pre-ART South Africa — "protective" alleles (HLA-B*57/B*58:01/B*81:01) were increasing in frequency while "susceptible" alleles were declining, a process driven by differential AIDS progression and vertical transmission. ART roll-out has dramatically slowed this evolutionary pressure: protective alleles showed a sustained increase from 23% to a projected 42% over 45 years without ART, but this trajectory is arrested in the ART era. Beyond its evolutionary significance, this study provides striking empirical evidence that widespread ART deployment alters the selective forces operating on human populations, with implications for long-term epidemic biology and HIV cure research contexts.

## 10. Hepatitis B Vaccination Histories in Persons With HIV Needing Revaccination

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Published** | 1 May 2026 |
| **Journal** | *Journal of Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndromes* |
| **Study type** | Tier 3 — Retrospective Study (A5379 Trial Sub-Analysis) |
| **Category** | 🟡 Implementation · 🟢 Prevention |
| **PMID** | [41452251](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41452251/) |
| **DOI** | [10.1097/QAI.0000000000003828](https://doi.org/10.1097/QAI.0000000000003828) |

**Summary:** This retrospective analysis of 561 participants in the A5379 trial (across 41 sites in Africa, Asia, South America, and the US) found that 24% of HIV-positive adults had received four or more prior hepatitis B vaccine doses yet still lacked seroprotective antibody titres (<10 mIU/mL), highlighting the inadequacy of standard dosing in this population. The study raises an urgent implementation point: long-acting ART regimens (CAB+RPV LA and lenacapavir-based) do not protect against HBV, unlike tenofovir-based oral ART. As LAI ART uptake increases, clinicians must proactively reassess HBV serology and ensure patients receive the CpG-adjuvanted vaccine series (which shows superior immunogenicity in PWH) to close this emerging prevention gap.

## Key Themes

**1. Long-acting antiretrovirals maturing across treatment and prevention.** Multiple articles this week advance the evidence base for LAI regimens: real-world US data (ABOVE Study) confirm superior adherence for CAB+RPV LA vs. oral ART; PK-PD modelling refines lenacapavir PrEP protective thresholds and resistance risks; and a Phase I study supports a once-weekly oral islatravir/lenacapavir FDC. Together they map a trajectory from injectable every-2-months to twice-yearly, with a possible once-weekly oral option on the horizon.

**2. Cardiovascular comorbidities increasingly central to HIV treatment.** The REPRIEVE trial continues to yield important secondary findings — this week linking pitavastatin to a 17% reduction in incident hypertension and confirming that new-onset hypertension more than doubles MACE risk. HIV treatment is no longer just about viral suppression; comprehensive cardiometabolic management is now integral to HIV care guidelines.

**3. Resistance and safety gaps in LAI scale-up require proactive management.** The lenacapavir PK/PD modelling study identifies a window during which resistance can emerge after discontinuation, while the HBV vaccination sub-study highlights that switching from tenofovir-based oral ART to LAI regimens removes incidental HBV protection. Both findings point to system-level gaps that clinicians and programmes must address as LAI regimens expand.

**4. Implementation disparities remain the limiting factor for impact.** Across settings — India's MSM/PWID communities, eight African countries' HIV testing programmes, and China's virologic failure management — structural barriers (stigma, fragmented services, lack of drug resistance testing, variable self-testing uptake) remain the primary obstacles. High acceptability of LAI regimens does not automatically translate to access; demand creation, flexible delivery, and health system integration are the current bottlenecks.
